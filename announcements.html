Business Announcements

There were occasions when partners needed to be proactively informed of upcoming changes to licensing rules or pricing. These business communications needed to be introduced into the portal.

TL;DR

Working closely with other members of the business that were involved with the portal I introduced a number of important announcements to inform partners of issues and changes to licensing as well as additional informative messaging relating to changes in the portal. I used a variety of components from the design library to introduce these business communicaitons including tooltips, information banners, modals and a blog section on the homepage.

1) Pricing Updates - Information Banners

It was brought to my attention by a member of the account management team that new rules on pricing was introduced as part of Microsoft's 'New Commerce Experience' for CSP programs. With NCE, Microsoft prices were going to be based on the location of the end customer but would be billed in the currency of the provider location. This was causing confusion for partners when they reviewed their invoices so they needed to be made aware of these changes. I worked with the business to introduce copy into an information banner and tested the introduction of this banner into the portal.

I introduced the banner displayed below, where the message was short and concise with a link for a more detailed summary included in the banner. The link would open a pdf document about these changes. Importantly the banner can be closed by the user if required so that it does not distract or become an annoyance. This message was included when the user was in the product list section about to buy a new license, in the customer creation page and also in the customer detail where applicable.

2) NCE Subscription Update - Homepage Modal

As part of Microsofts NCE (New Commerce Experience) program all legacy subscriptions had to be migrated to an NCE license by July. Partners would have to proactively manage this migration for their customer subscriptions. This information was important to communicate to partners as without a migration the legacy subscriptions would not renew, and would expire.

As this message was so important to communicate I decided to introduce it into the portal as soon as a partner logs in. The screen is blocked by a modal which the user can close off. Importantly a checkbox is included so that the partner can close this message without having to view the modal again with the message being unchecked by default. The message was also added to the blog section on the homepage (shown in a later design).
