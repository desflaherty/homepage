Subscription Renewals

Using customer feedback and working with the business to introduce subscription status changes and information for partners to be informed about customer subscription renewals and expiry dates.

TL;DR

From speaking to customers on preview calls and from working closely with business development managers it became evident that partners needed a better way of managing their customer subscriptions. They wanted to be informed of when these subscriptions were set to renew or when they were expiring or had expired. I worked with the DBMS, and the backend and frontend development teams to design a solution that would meet partners needs.

Subscriptions List (Before Re-design)

The subscriptions list was used by partners to view the subscriptions they had bought on behalf of their customers. Just as SoftwareONE was a Microsoft reseller, partners that used the ONEClub partner portal also acted as resellers, purchasing licenses on behalf of their customers. The subscriptions list below shows the subscription status, product name, quantity, price, customer name and subscription end date. What was missing here was a way for partners to clearly see which subscriptions were on auto-renew, when subscriptions were set to renew and also which subscriptions were expiring soon or had already expired.

Subscriptions List (Design Iteration 1)

The screenshot below shows a mockup design for proposed improvements to the subscriptions list page. There were a number of suggestions made including;

  • Badges to show whether the auto-renewal toggle switch in the subscriptions detail page was on or off.
  • New status indicators for the subscriptions to show if subscriptions were expiring soon or have expired already.
  • Badges to display the number of days left before the subscription renews or expires, to be displayed under the subscription end date.

Subscriptions List (Design Iteration 2)

After the mockup proposal was tested early feedback was that the design was overly complex and too much noise was in the design. It was felt that there was too many different colours and the badges were a distraction as opposed to being helpful for the user. A later design as shown below removed the coloured badges, but kept the information to show the user when the subscription would renew or expire. The status indicators received positive feedback and these were kept in the design as users felt they were needed in order to clearly see which subscriptions were expiring soon at a glance. The 'Auto-renews' and 'Expires' information was shown on the top line in the End Date column with the date below it as this was information was the most important for the user to see.
