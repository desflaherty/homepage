Product Catalogue

To improve the loading time for the products and to make them more accessible to partners the product catalogue was improved. Most relevant products were displayed to partners based on their previous purchasing history.

TL;DR

The original design for the product catalogue loaded the full catalogue whenever the partner used the 'Buy' button on the homepage or 'Buy' link from the main navigation menu. The product catalogue consists of over 1,200 products and the full catalogue was loaded for every partner, dislpaying the products in no particular order or relevance to the partner. Also, because all products were being loaded there could be a delay which resulted in a spooing wheel being shown to the partner - this was a negative experience. A more intelligent approach was needed.

Data Driven Design

Qualitative Research - Speaking to our partners

From partner preview calls it became evident that the current method of loading the full product catalogue was causing frustration for partners. At times they had to wait as the full catalogue was being loaded. Also the products being displayed to them were not personalised, they were being shown products that they had no interest in purchasing. They then had to use the product filters or the product search menu to find a particular product. In most cases partners purchased the same products repeatedly, so having to filter or search every time for a product was not the best experience and also took more effort and time for them.

Partner Feedback on Existing Catalogue

Quantitative Research - User Journeys from Product Detail

Using the new 'user journeys' feature in Heap I wanted to analyse the conversion from the product detail page directly into the shopping cart. So where users were in the product detail and then clicked on 'Buy Now' vs users who were in the product detail and moved away from this page to return to the product catalogue before then purchasing.

A large number of users moved from the product detail back into the catalogue and this suggested to me that they might not have initially found the product they were looking for so were searching the catalogue again. This data also validated the proposed design change to the catalogue as the new design would show users the products most relevent to them so they should not have to search the catalogue as often and should be able to move directly into the shopping cart once they have the product they need.

User Journeys from Product Detail to Shopping Cart

Proposed Design - Personalising the Product Catalogue

The negative feedback from partners lead to a proposed re-design to the product catalogue. Filters were introduced as shown in the mockups below. A 'Favourite' chip wpuld filter by products that the partners purchase most frequently, based on their order history. 'Most Recent' displays the last products that were purchased by the partner. 'Popular' would filter the catalogue by the products that are purchased most frequently by all partners. Finally 'Full Catalogue' would allow the partner to load the full catalogue if still required.
