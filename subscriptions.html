Customer Subscriptions

One of the most important job functions for the partners was to be able to easily navigate to their customer list so they could upsize their customer subscriptions. This improvement reduced the number of clicks needed to do this task.

TL;DR

From analysing the clicks against the main navigation cards on the homepage it became quickly evident that the 'View Customers' card was being used the most by partners. Observing this quantitative data lead me to investigate further during customer preview calls. I enquired about the main job functions that partners were doing when they were using the platform and they mentioned that they navigated to their customers so that they could upsize their customer subscriptions from the customer detail page. I investigated if we could reduce the number of clicks needed by bringing customer subscriptions up a level to the customer list - thus reducing the number of clicks needed. So this design improvement used quantitative and qualitative research to implement an improvement to the existing functionality, reducing the number of clicks needed to accomplish an important task for partners.

Data Driven Design

Quantitative Research - Homepage Navigation Cards

The 'View Customers' card on the homepage had the highest engagement out of the navigation cards shown here. Using 'Heap Analytics' clicks against each of the homepage cards was obtained. The 'View Customers' card had 66% of the engagement across these cards in the last 30 days.

Qualitative Research - Speaking to our partners

I wanted to know more about the high engagement on the 'View Customers' card. The best way to understand why this card was being interacted with so much was to speak to partners. They informed me that one of their main job functions was to upsize their customer subscriptions. The path that was used to perform this job function was to navigate to the customers list and then to click on a customer to go into the customer detail. Once inside the customer detail they clicked on a filter that scrolled the page to that customers subscriptions. From here they could click on the active subscriptions filter that navigated to the subscriptions page with those subscriptions selected. They could then upsize from here using the quantity increase.

Design Improvement - Modifying the Customer List

It made sense to introduce the active subscriptions filter to the customers list. Making it accessible from the list would mean partners would not have to click into the customer detail, and then click on the customer subscriptions tab within the detail page. So the design improvement would remove two clicks that were currently needed to access this filter. Partners would now be be able to filter on their customers subscriptions from their customer list and then upsize the filtered subscriptions.

Feature Adoption - Analysis

Engagement on the subscription filters that were introduced to the customer list page page showed almost 1,000 clicks since deployment from Aug-Dec 2022. The engagement with the filters that are in the customer detail page is still a lot higher however, which shows that users are still using this method to filter on their customer subscriptions even though a quicker method has been introduced. This may be due to users already having an established pattern of behaviour in place, and it could take some time for them to adopt a new method. The analysis for the adoption of this new feature will be run again in the future to see if adoption increases further.

Qualitative feedback was also gathered from partners on this new feature, and was positive, as can be seen by the comments taken from partners below. 'A quick jump', 'Amazing', 'Perfect'.
