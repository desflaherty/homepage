Equinox Cafe

Graphic Design

The Brief

The task was to visit the National Museum of Archaeology and carry out research that would lead to the development of a new logo and branding material for the Museum cafe.

Concept Development

When visiting the National Museum a number of elements and designs stood out to me that could potentially be used in the design piece for a logo. In particular I found the early Celtic symbols and monuments to be of interest to me so I wanted to incorporate these into my design. During this exploratory phase I noticed the spirals that were carved into the monument at Newgrange that symbolise the sun and researching the monument I discovered that it was built to capture the rising sun during the spring equinox when the sun would illuminate the passageway of the monument. The idea of using ‘Equinox’ as the name of the café came to mind and researching symbolic meaning behind the equinox I discovered that it represented a time of renewal and rebirth and of reconnecting with the world and feeling rejuvenated and refreshed. These feelings also overlap with the benefits and reasons of why people drink fruit juice and coffee so the name seemed very suitable to use for a café logo. Once I had decided the name for the café I began idea generation in my sketchbook writing out the word ‘Equinox’ to see what symbols could be used. I then began development of various ideas using Adobe Illustrator.

I wanted to tie in the theme of a café into the logo so the idea of using a coffee cup came to mind. I then identified three final ideas from my designs that I wanted to explore in greater detail. The final idea I chose uses the coffee cup symbol in place of the letter ‘o’ in the wording of the logo so the final logo now had elements of the spring equinox and also the theme of coffee represented..
