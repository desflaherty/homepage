Equinox Cafe

Graphic Design

The Brief

The task was to visit the National Museum of Archaeology and carry out research that would lead to the development of a new logo and branding material for the Museum cafe.

Concept Development

When visiting the National Museum a number of elements and designs stood out that could potentially be used in the design piece for a logo. The early Celtic symbols and monuments were of interest and studied to incorporate into the design. During this exploratory phase the spirals that were carved into the monument at Newgrange that symbolise the sun were noticed and researching the monument lead to the discovered that it was built to capture the rising sun during the spring equinox when the sun would illuminate the passageway of the monument. The idea of using ‘Equinox’ as the name of the café was discovered and when researching the symbolic meaning behind the equinox it was discovered that it represented a time of renewal and rebirth and of reconnecting with the world and feeling rejuvenated and refreshed. These feelings also overlap with the benefits and reasons of why people drink fruit juice and coffee so the name seemed very suitable to use for a café logo. Once the name was decided for the café idea generation was begun uses sketches and writing out the word ‘Equinox’ to see what symbols could be used. Development of various ideas using Adobe Illustrator began.

An eclipse was incorporated into the logo idea also the theme of a café was brought into the logo by using the icon of a coffee cup. Different colour schemes were experimented with. The colour needed to represent the spring sun while also representing coffee. A gradient using orange and yellow to represent the sun was developed and this colour scheme worked well as the warm colours are inviting and would attract the attention of someone that was in need of warming and refreshing themselves by buying a coffee. The warm orange colour is also suitable as it triggers the idea of a refreshing breakfast, buying an orange juice to drink before you face the challenges of a new day. Two final ideas from the designs were identified and explored in greater detail.

The final logo design keeps the eclipse symbol but also uses the celtic spiral in place of the letter 'q' in the logo name. A coffee cup is used in place of the 'o' letter and a gradient effect is used on these symbols. The rest of the logo lettering is in a brown colour to reinforce the café subject and this colour is also associated with the earth so fits in well with the overall theme of the design. The final design is produced in grey scale also to ensure the design works well in black and white according to good design principles.

Branding material was also developed to accompany the new logo. This took the form of an A4 letterhead, a business card and a compliment slip. An A3 sized poster to promote the opening of the new cafe was also created.
