Fly UX

A user-friendly flight booking desktop website.

My role: UX Research & Design

Scroll To Top

01

Overview

'Fly UX' is a flight booking website for desktop. The objective was to Conduct research on existing flight booking websites identify pain points users were experiencing, and use the findings to design a new solution that would overcome these frustrations and lead to a better user experience in the flight booking process.

The prototype was created following the UX design process including:

  • Phase 1 - Usability Testing, Depth Interviews, Competitor Analysis, Online Surveys.
  • Phase 2 - Affinity Diagram, Customer Journey Diagram, Insight Generation.
  • Phase 3 - Desktop Flow Diagram, Define Navigation, Desktop Interaction Design.
  • Phase 4 - Wireframes & Prototype Build.

The main tools used for this project were:

  • Pen & Paper.
  • Camtasia (Video Editing)
  • Sketch.
  • Invision.

02

User Research

Usability Testing

The first assignment undertaken was to conduct two usability tests to obtain some qualitative data about the usability of existing airline websites. I began by defining my research objectives to determine what I wanted to achieve from the tests. I created a recruitment screener, a consent form and a test script to use during the desktop usability tests.The test script was designed ensuring not to use leading or closed questions as these would impact the quality of the data obtained. I conducted two desktop usability tests using two different test subjects on the Aer Lingus and Ryanair websites.

Test Objectives:

  1. Identify typical user behaviour on the Aer Lingus & Ryanair websites when booking a flight.
  2. Discover the primary goals of the user.
  3. Understand 'best in class' features being used by the airlines.
  4. Understand features that cause the user to be frustrated.
  5. Identify pain points that could cause the user to leave the website.
  6. Identify features and areas on the website that could be improved for better UX.

Usability Testing Screenshots

Competitor Analysis

I reviewed four websites to identify what best in class competitor websites were currently doing well. I wanted to gain more insight into how these websites were solving problems that users face when booking flights, understand current conventions that should be followed and highlight best practice being used.

Online Survey

I created an online survey using Survey Monkey to gather more qualitative and quantitative attitudinal research to gain more understanding into context of use. I used a mixture of open and closed questions as well as some multiple choice questions. In keeping with good practice the survey contained no more than 10 questions and would take around 2-3 minutes for people to complete.

03

Analysis & Planning

Affinity Diagram

I reviewed the usability tests and recorded notes on the test sessions. I then wrote down every important point that was mentioned onto separate post-it notes. The depth interview, competitor analysis and data gathered from the online survey was also reviewed and post-it notes were developed from this research also. The notes were then stuck on to a whiteboard and organised into categories. These categories were used to create the affinity map as shown below.

Customer Journey Diagram

The next step was to use the insights obtained from the Affinity Map to create the Customer Journey diagram. I took the insights and organised them into a step by step view of the customer journey through the airline websites to demonstrate how the customer felt at each stage of the process. I noted the customers goals, behaviours, context and pain points at each step of the journey. Icons were used to demonstrate how the customer felt during the process, whether they were happy, frustrated or upset.

Insight Generation

From the work completed up to this point some insights emerged from the research methods in regard to users frustrations and pain points. It was evident that users wanted:

  1. More clarity around the price being paid for the flights before they reached the checkout point in the process.
  2. Users were also being frustrated by the addition of add ons and upsell messages being displayed on the existing websites researched.
  3. A guest checkout option was something that users would avail of as users didn't want to be forced to login or sign up in order to purchase the flight.
  4. Site functionality could also be improved on, namely when using the calendar or to find new dates using the arrow buttons on the search results page.

04

Design

Desktop Flow Diagram

I defined a high level flow for the desktop website with the objective of fixing any issues that I would have discovered during the research phase and highlighted on the customer journey map. The task for the project was to focus on one flow or primary use case. I initially sketched out the diagram before using Sketch to create the flow.

Define Navigation

I began sketching out some initial concepts for the airline navigation. On the homepage there would be primary and secondary navigation menus that would be hidden using a hamburger menu icon once the user has searched for flights. The hidden menu links would be available through the booking process until the user enters the checkout screens where a status bar would display the steps needed to complete the payment process. There would be an easy option to skip all add ons in order to complete the process as quickly as possible.

Desktop Interaction Design

My objectives for this part of the process were to sketch the screens and the screen states for the users flowing through the flight booking website addressing all the issues and user goals identified previously in my research. Interactions would have included predictive fields where the airport name populates when 3 characters have been entered into the search form, the cheapest price for a flight being clearly highlighted, being able to use the arrow buttons to search for a new flight without errors appearing, simple skip button to avoid add on screens, a guest checkout option and a total fare cost summary being displayed before checkout.

Phase 4: Prototype & Wireframe
Medium Fidelity Prototype, Wireframe

Medium Fidelity Prototype:

I designed each screen using Sketch in Medium Fidelity form to create a prototype. This prototype could then be tested and further developed before creating the Wireframes. I ensured that the prototype contained enough detail and interactivity to test the high level flow, the screen layouts, text and basic interactions. The finished prototype was brought into InVision to test the interaction design.

Wireframes:

The final stage in the process was to develop the wireframes. At this point research, analysis, design and prototyping have been completed but before handing over to the software development team wireframes with specific detail need to be included. The wireframes should include all the necessary specifics so that the developers are clear on how to code the site and will not need to revert with questions or queries.

Course Review:

The course provided me with an in-depth understanding of the UX research and design process. Going forward I would feel confident in my ability to prepare and conduct usability tests and interviews and to also analyze the output from them. During the course I also improved upon my software skills and knowledge by using programs such as Sketch, InVision and Adobe Illustrator. I would feel comfortable using these tools to create industry ready Diagrams, Prototypes and Wireframes in any future projects that I am involved in.
