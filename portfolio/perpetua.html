Perpetua Font

Print

The Brief

Research a given typeface and produce an A3 poster to promote that typeface. The research should look into the history of the typeface - its designer and any other relevant information. The poster should visually indicate unique characters of that typeface along with any other relevant pieces of information necessary to the promotion of that typeface.

Concept Development

I initially undertook some research into the font to help formulate an idea and come up with a theme that could be used in the project. I discovered that the font was created in 1925 by Eric Gill who was a leading English sculptor and stonemason at the time. The Perpetua font was used a lot in tombstones and war memorials which is the reason that I chose the idea of a war memorial for the final project piece.

The colour scheme I wanted to use for the poster was determined by the theme of the war memorial so grey, black and white were my initial ideas. I also wanted to use a stone effect for the background to re-enforce the theme. The imagery used in the poster are developed from the characters in the Perpetua font typeface. The letter ‘T’ from the Perpetua font is used to represent a cross, I used different size lettering here also to give the poster a feeling of depth. The idea of creating tree branches from lettering in the font was developed as viewed on the right side of the poster. Animal shapes fitting with the theme of a graveyard and Irish war memorial were developed to take the form of lizards and snakes. A celtic cross and a soldiers medal were developed as seen in the top left and right of the poster. A stone effect for the background was created using a gradient effect. A blood red colour is used for the font name and it is also randomly added to some letters on the tombstone to give a blood spattered effect.
