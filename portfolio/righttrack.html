Right Track

A job searching tool, that organises and keeps track of your job applications.

My role: UX/UI Design

Overview

‘Right Track’ is a tool that simplifies the job search and tracking process. The user can search from multiple job boards within the app and also save their job search results and applications to a Kanban board that provides an easy way of staying organised and on track.

Scroll To Top

Project Background

After finishing an intensive UI Design bootcamp I was actively looking for a new role. The longer I spent on job boards and looking for a job the more frustrations I discovered. I found it time consuming to be moving between a variety of different job board websites using the same keyword searches where the job results being returned were largely the same - as employers and recruiters were advertising the same role accross various job boards - but the job seeker had to check every board anyway just in case they missed a job ad!

Keeping track of applications also proved to be a pain point - and I found myself using an Excel spreadsheet to record applications.

I felt like these problems could be solved by the right app being designed so decided to undertake a project using a user centered design approach to find out if the pain points and frustrations I felt were also being experienced by other job seekers.

Competitor Analysis

I investigated what products already existed on the market that might address the problem that I was experiencing and carried out a competitor analysis. One of the products I looked at was called 'Huntr' a job tracking app designed in the States.

Huntr

Strengths: • One of the few apps specifically dedicated to job tracking. There’s many project management tools out there, but most need to be ‘hacked’ into a job search template or model. • Nice UI – web app has useful features such as a Kanban board. • Has a web app as well as iOS. • Changes made in the mobile will update in the web app they work together. • Has an extension to add to the browser.

Weaknesses: • The app will only save the job feed for search results and not the specific job that you are viewing. • The web app doesn’t return many listings for Dublin – a keyword search for ‘ux designer’ returned only 4 jobs. • Some of the jobs displayed are expired on the company board. • The web app can’t filter by job boards in the search. • The web app isn’t user friendly on mobile, it’s clunky and cluttered. The navigation is squashed and at the top of the screen away from a user’s thumbs that makes it harder to use.

Opportunities: • The web app does not find many local job listings and displays expired ads. It is designed with a US market in mind. My web app could contain Irish listings and be focused on the Irish market. • The web app has usability issues on mobile. • The native app version has usability issues when adding searched jobs. • Only offers a limited amount of jobs (40) that can be tracked on the free version.

Threats: • The UI of the app is nice and there are some nice features. However the app has usability issues and a better app could be introduced to the market. • Project management apps such as Asana or Atlassian could introduce a better version with marketing support.
