Problem Statement

Unemployment makes people unhappy. It has been associated with over 100 psychological variables. The current COVID-19 crisis has greatly added to the number of those unemployed in Ireland. The current tools available for finding jobs such as job boards and company job pages are adding to job seekers stress and frustrations.

How do I know this is a real problem?

CSO data for April 2020 showed that there were 1.3 million people in receipt of an unemployment related social welfare payment in Ireland.

Unemployment has been associated with over 100 psychological variables such as low mood, anxiety, poor cognitive performance, loss of confidence, and psychosomatic problems.

Studies undertaken have found frustrations users experience with well known job boards include irrelevant job matches, difficulty tracking job applications, poor filtering options, and duplicated job ads .

Primary Research

As a user centered design approach is being used I wanted to investigate the topic further by carrying out some of my own primary research. I created a survey using 'Survey Monkey' to obtain quantitative data on some of my potential product users. I obtained twenty six respondents for the online survey. The participants were sourced from my bootcamp slack channel where students have finsihed the bootcamp and are now beginning the job search process. They were also not currently in full time employment. The online survey provided some interesting information about users behaviour and frustrations when undertaking a job search. In order to find out more information on these frustrations I conducted some interviews with potential users of my app, gathering qualitative data that will will help me to gain further understanding on the topic.

How will I know if I've solved this problem?

I intend on creating an interactive prototype for a new job searching tool that I will test with users. As users are using an equal mix of apps and desktop I will be designing a web app, but will be adopting a mobile first approach. I will record my findings from the usability tests and if the users are able to use my prototype to quickly search for a job and find it to be an enjoyable experience then I will determine that I have created a better job searching and tracking tool when compared to existing products.

User Personas

From analysing the data gathered from previous research a clear picture of the apps potential users emerged. User personas were then developed based on the patterns and similarities between my interview participants. These personas will help me to understand the user and will inform future design decisions.

Task Model

I created a task model to understand what users would want from my app and how I could go about designing it. The model was used to determine what steps users will take when using the app and the key decisions they need to make while using it.

Customer Experience Map

A customer experience map was then designed as a tool for visualizing all of the journeys a customer of my app may experience, as well as a visual of how all of these journeys are connected. The map will help me to determine where problems might be occurring within the customer’s overall experience. By mapping out a customer’s entire product experience, any problem areas can be highlighted and visualized. I designed the customer experience map for one of the personas I had created - in this example for the 'Unemployed Job Seeker'.

Information Architecture

A card sorting exercise was then conducted to evaluate and generate ideas for the information architecture of the app. The aim was to find patterns that give clues about the most logical and user-friendly way to organize my app. I involved participants using 'OptimalWorkshop' as the methodology and employed an open card sort approach. Next a site map was created. The map acts as a visual aid to see the relationships between the pages and content of the app and ensures that the information I've collected and categorized is connected with a strong flow.

User Stories

User stories were then developed showing short, simple descriptions of the features the app will need from the perspective of my user. They followed the template of "As < type of user >, I want < some goal > so that < some reason >."

As someone that is unemployed I want to be able to search for a new job so that I can make an application.

As someone that is unemployed I want to be able to apply to a saved job so that I can get hired.

User Flows

Based on all the information learned in the research stage as well as assessing what features the app would provide for users I mapped out the primary screens of the app from registration to end goal. The user stories were translated into flows to represent a visual journey through the app for each user task identified. It was important to make the user flow intuitively so that it was a simple process from beginning to end. Each user flow had an entry point and a success criteria. Sketches

I then began the ideation phase of sketching out screen ideas for the different users flows in the app. The plan for this phase was to create a paper prototype then test with users to iterate the design before moving on to develop high fidelity wireframes.

I examined the data collected from the user research and also my user personas when brainstorming ideas. One of the comments that came from users needs was "An app like a Trello board just for the job search topic". I investigated the Trello app and noticed that a kanban board was being used to organise content. I decided that a similar idea could be used for my app for the job tracker screen where saved jobs would be displayed so sketched out this idea. Other user needs were taken into account when deciding on possible options for the navigation menu.

A problem with testing the low-fi prototype

I created a paper prototype and again reached out to my slack channel group for testing and feedback. Unfortunately I found it hard to find students to test the low fidelity prototype. It was at a time when students were under pressure to get assignments completed and the fact that my design was in low fidelity format also contributed to the lack of interest I feel. At this point I was stuck. I decided the only thing I could do to progress the project was to develop the wireframes to a high fidelity format and then return to the slack channel at a later point and hope for better engagement.

High Fidelity Wireframes

I chose 'Figma' as the software tool to use for the high fidelity wireframes and for the prototype development. To manage the transition from paper to digital protoype, I created greyscale wireframes and then developed these into high fidelity. I developed a working prototype of some of the basic user flows from linking the wireframes together so that I could test these with my end users. Prototype Testing - Take 2

I returned to my slack channel and this time managed to engage with my target users for feedback. I tested the prototype with five participants using a 0-2 completion scale. In general feedback from users was that they found the app to be intuitive and easy to use. They noted that it was a quicker and more efficent way of searching for job ads and finding the relevant ads that they could apply to.

  • 0: User can’t perform the task.
  • 1: User performed task but had some hesitation.
  • 2: User can perform the task quickly and with no trouble.

Insights

Some additions and refinements were made to the app based on comments from users during testing.

  • Onboarding screens were added to clearly explain the features of the app to users.
  • Feedback regarding interface design amendments were taken on board and improvements were made to the usability and accessibility of the app.
  • The size of the remove duplicates button was increased as it is the primary button that the users choose initially.
  • The filter button was replaced by a filter icon that is highlighted in green to increase its visability.
  • The size of the heading that displays the number of job titles was increased to improve accessibility.

Future proposed testing

I intend to carry out further testing on the app before development. I would like to carry out moderated usability testing to record how long it would take a user to find and apply to a job using my prototype and run a comparison test with an existing job board app such as Irishjobs or LinkedIn. These tests would provide quantitative data that would prove the value of the features that are provided in my app, such as being able to search from multiple job boards without having to move across different browser windows or apps when searching and also the ability to match exactly to the job title looked for by using the additional filter options, or to exclude duplicated jobs being advertised by recruitment agencies.
