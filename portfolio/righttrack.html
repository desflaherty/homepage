Right Track

A job searching tool that organises and keeps track of your job applications.

My role: UX/UI Design

01

Overview

‘Right Track’ is a tool that simplifies the job search and tracking process. The user can search from multiple job boards within the app and also save their job search results and applications to a kanban board that provides an easy way of staying organised and on track.

Scroll To Top

Project Background

After finishing an intensive UI Design bootcamp I was actively looking for a new role. The longer I spent on job boards and looking for a job the more frustrations I discovered. I found it time consuming to be moving between a variety of different job board websites using the same keyword searches where the job results being returned were largely the same - as employers and recruiters were advertising the same role accross various job boards - but the job seeker had to check every board anyway just in case they missed a job ad!

Keeping track of applications also proved to be a pain point - and I found myself using an Excel spreadsheet to record applications.

I felt like these problems could be solved by the right app being designed so decided to undertake a project using a user centered design approach to find out if the pain points and frustrations I felt were also being experienced by other job seekers.

Problem Definition

  • Searching for jobs accross multiple job boards takes time and is frustrating.
  • Tracking saved jobs and applications becomes difficult the longer the job search takes.
  • Keyword search results on existing job boards return inaccurate job ads as they are not matched to the job title.

02

Discovery

Competitor Analysis

I investigated what products already existed on the market that might address the problem that I was experiencing and carried out a competitor analysis. One of the products I looked at was called 'Huntr' a job tracking app designed in the States.

Huntr Job Tracker

Other Products

Products that are available for organising tasks using a kanban board include 'Trello', 'Asana' and 'Jira'. These tools are intended for collaboration mainly and have no job searching function. They could be used as organisers for job tracking however. Job searching apps include 'IrishJobs', 'LinkedIn', 'Monster', and 'Glassdoor'. These tools have limited fucnitonality for tracking job applications and each tool has to be used separately.

User Research

Online Survey

As a user centered design approach is being used I carried out an online survey using 'Survey Monkey' to obtain quantitative data on my target audience. There were a total of twenty six respondents for the online survey. The participants were sourced from my bootcamp slack channel where students have finsihed the bootcamp and are now beginning the job search process. I wanted to find out more information on how they go about their job search process and uncover any frustrations and pain points they were experiencing. User Interviews

The online survey provided some interesting information about users behaviour and frustrations when undertaking a job search. In order to find out more information on these frustrations I conducted some interviews with potential users of my app , gathering qualitative data that will will help me to gain further understanding on the topic.

03

Concepting

User Personas

From analysing the data gathered from previous research a clear picture of the apps potential users emerged. User personas were then developed based on the patterns and similarities between my interview participants. These personas will help me to understand the user and will inform future design decisions.

Previous Next

Task Model

I created a task model to understand what users would want from my app and how I could go about designing it. The model was used to determine what steps users will take when using the app and the key decisions they need to make while using it.

Customer Experience Map

A customer experience map was then designed as a tool for visualizing all of the journeys a customer of my app may experience, as well as a visual of how all of these journeys are connected. The map will help me to determine where problems might be occurring within the customer’s overall experience. By mapping out a customer’s entire product experience, any problem areas can be highlighted and visualized. I designed the customer experience map for one of the personas I had created - in this example for the 'Career Changer'.

Remote Card Sorting

A card sorting exercise was then conducted to evaluate and generate ideas for the information architecture of the app. The aim was to find patterns that give clues about the most logical and user-friendly way to organize my app. I involved participants using 'OptimalWorkshop' as the methodology and employed an open card sort approach. Participants were asked to add the app content into their own categories, then to name these categories.

Site Map

Next a site map was created. The goal here was to reflect the IA patterns of the product by laying out the page hierarchy of the application. From the homepage the users can navigate to six different sections. There are also sub sections under these. The map acts as a visual aid to see the relationships between the pages and content of the app and ensures that the information I've collected and categorized is connected with a strong flow. The sitemap will also be used as a reference point when designing the wireframes later in the process.

User Stories

User stories were then developed showing short, simple descriptions of the features the app will need from the perspective of my user. They follow the template of "As < type of user >, I want < some goal > so that < some reason >."

  1. As someone that needs a new job I want to be able to search for jobs using the filter options and save the job so that I can keep track of my saved jobs.
  2. As someone that has saved a job I want to be able to apply to the job so that I will be contacted by the company for an interview.
  3. As someone that has been contacted for an interview I want to be able to save an interview date so that I won't forget the interview.

05

User Testing

I tested the prototype with five participants and there were a number of improvements that were made to the design based on their feedback. Changes made included improvements to the user interface design and user experience concerns regarding usability and accessibility of the app were also addressed.

Job Search Results
  • To improve usability the 'remove duplicates' and filter button were changed. The size of the 'remove duplicates' button was increased as it is the primary button that the users choose initially.

  • The filter button was replaced by a filter icon that is highlighted in green to increase its visability.

  • The size of the heading that displays the number of job titles was increased to improve accessibility.

  • Users felt that the cards displaying the job listing were too heavy, so the border was removed.

  • The job title was being displayed in green on the card but this was confusing to users as it looked like an action, so the colour for job title was changed to grey.

  • The plus symbol used to add the job listing to the Kanban board was yellow initially but this was changed to green as it is an action that the user can perform.

Sign In to LinkedIn
  • Another concern was rasied regarding the 'Sign In to LinkedIn' screen. It was felt that this was also confusing as it was not clear to users what they were being asked to do on this screen. A LinkedIn logo was being used but was replaced by a 'Sign In to LinkedIn' heading to clarify to the user what they needed to do on this screen.

  • The hidden password icon was being displayed in a yellow colour initially but this was changed to green as green is the primary colour being used for actions.

  • For consistency the order that the primary and secondary buttons was changed here also.

  • The bevel on buttons was reduced from 20px down to 10px.

  • The border has been removed from the cards, and the job title font colour has been changed from green to grey as the title is not a link or an action.
