One Day

A health and wellness app that aims to improve a users mental and physical wellbeing by promoting a change to their daily habits and routines.

My role: UI Design

01

Overview

‘One Day’ is a health and wellness mobile app that aims to improve a users overall mental and physical wellbeing by enabling them to make positive changes to their daily habits and behaviours. The app will be a resource that will promote healthy eating, provide sleep advice, and improve mental and physical health through stress reduction methods and techniques.

The mobile app was created following iOS guidelines for iPhone and a material design version for Android was also developed.

The main tools used for this project were:

  • Balsamiq.
  • Sketch.
  • Invision.

Scroll To Top

Objective

There are a number of related apps on the market currently that provide similar content however my app will be different to these apps as it will encompass elements of both mental and physical health within the one app while also adopting a more simplified approach focusing on each part of a users day that they can change to bring about better health.

Content will be provided for a morning routine, a daily commute and an evening routine to encourage new habits that promote both mental and physical wellbeing.

Key features proposed:

  • Morning Routine Section - Be able to choose from breakfast suggestions, stretching exercises, meditation, view helpful tips and suggestions.
  • Commuting Section - Be able to listen to podcasts, listen to relaxing music, view relaxing imagery and motivational content.
  • Evening Routine Section - Be able to view content on sleep, meditation, exercise, outdoor activities, healthy evening meals, tips to unwind.
  • User Profile Section - Allow a user to set up a profile to view their favourite content from their activity on the app.

02

User Flows

The requirement for the project was to design two versions for the mobile app, one for iOS and one for Android. The brief stated to create three user flows that will detail how users will complete particular tasks in the app, taking care not to make the app too complicated as there will need to be two verisons designed.

User Story 1

“As a user I want to be able to view beautiful images of nature when I am commuting to work”

User Story 2

“As a user I want to be able to listen to a sleepcast before I go to bed”

03

Wireframes

The user flows formed the foundation for initial low fidelity wireframes using balsamiq. These initial wireframes were designed for iOS bearing in mind iOS guidelines. At this stage sketching using paper and pencil would have also been appropriate but because I had not used the balsamiq software yet I wanted to use this low fidelity wireframing approach. The screens that were developed gave me an initial idea as to how the elements would be placed and what content would be included in the app as well as considerations such as the navigation elements - footer menu , tabs, user search form etc.

High Fidelity Wireframes

After initial wireframes were developed a further phase involved researching similar apps on the web to determine features and elements that could be incorporated into my mobile app also. During this phase of iteration guidelines for iOS and Android development were researched and this led to the development of high fidelity wireframes.

Careful consideration was paid to the end users of the app during the visual design process. Areas of design that were researched during this phase included; Colour theory and principles, UI trends, Typography, Flat Design & Material Design and Iconography.

Iterations based on feedback from my Tutor lead to adjustments and improvements in the design choices. Some screens were dropped as it was decided they were not needed, also a more consistent style was developed in terms of the layout of the content. Design choices were improved on also as can be seen in the 'Stretching' screen where the use of card elements to display different areas of the human body was replaced by a skeletal muscle image where the user could click on different body parts to find a video tutorial.

I developed the idea to use different colours for the different sections within the app - 'Home', 'Morning', 'Commuting' and 'Evening'. It seemed appropriate to use warm colours such as yellow and orange for the morning section to promote the feeling of energy and freshness aligned with the sun, and the evening section uses a relaxing darker colour, in this case purple and blue, which have associations with spirituality and calmness.

04

Usability Testing

The next phase was to create a prototype that could be tested with users for feedback. Based on this feedback changes were made to the designs before being finalised. The overall feedback from users that tested the app was positive. They liked the idea to use colours to identify different moments of the day. They felt that the screens were well designed and that the styling worked well with the theme, making them feel relaxed. They also felt that the intention of the app was clear and that they knew how to use it from the screens presented. Some constructive criticism led to some design changes as outlined below.

One pain point for users of the app seemed to be the 'Commuting' screen. They felt that the tabs at the top of the screen looked wrong and they would have preferred the screen design to have the same style as the 'Morning' screen, with cards used for the different selection choices. This feedback was taken on board and the screen was redesigned as can be seen below.

Before Testing

After Testing

For the 'Log In' and 'Sign up' screens there was some useful feedback from users also. They noted that the 'Sign Up' button blended into the background image so was not easily visible, and as this is a call to action button it should contrast with the background to be easily identified. The colour of the 'Sign Up' button was changed from dark grey to orange so that it was more visible. Users found the fields on the ‘Log In and ‘Sign Up’ screens a bit hard to see and noted that the background image was a distraction.The solution that was used was to lower the opacity of the background image and use a white input box behind the 'Log In' and 'Sign Up' fields so that they stood out more. Another piece of feedback noted that the screen looked a bit bare, so the logo design was incorporated into these screens.

Final Wireframes

Based on the feedback from the usability testing the design was modified and the final screens were developed. The changes reflected improvements to the spacing and alignment of screens and the 'Log In' and 'Sign Up' screens were adjusted as mentioned. In addition the tabs for Android were changed to be more in line with material design, where an underline with text is used as opposed to a rounded button style. Additional screens were also developed in the final design to include an error message screen to capture a user entering their 'Log In' details incorrectly, and a tooltip for the 'Sign Up' screen that gives feedback to a user regarding the strength of their password during sign up. Also an 'empty state' screen for when a user enters an invalid search choice was created to demonstrate how this feedback would appear to the user.

06

Retrospective

What went well?

Early research into existing apps as well as studying UI elelments that related to iOS and Android helped in the design of the wireframes. The usability tests yielded some constructive feedback that I was able to use to improve the final screens.

Skills:

Wireframes, Usability Testing.

Processes:

User Centered Design.

Learnings:

Research design sites and guidelines online. Engage with end users for design feedback.

What was a challenge?

This was the first time that I designed native screens for Android and iOS so becoming aware of the design guidelines used was a learning curve. Some of the early screens I designed had to be created again as they did not fit the material design recommendations.

Skills:

Material Design.

Processes:

User Centered Design.

Learnings:

Research native guidelines for iOS & Android further.

What can I improve on?

I was inspired for this project by existing apps such as 'Calm' which informed a lot of my design choices. In future I would like to develop screens that are a bit more unique and different to existing products. I feel that I am becoming more confident with my design choices so expect this will come with additional experience.

Skills:

Sketching, Wireframing.

Processes:

Design Iteration & Sketching.

Learnings:

Keep updated on latest trends and design styles, experiment with sketching and wireframe creation.
