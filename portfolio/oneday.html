One Day

A health and wellness app that aims to improve a users mental and physical wellbeing by promoting a change to their daily habits and routines.

My role: UI Design

Scroll To Top

01

Overview

‘One Day’ is a health and wellness mobile app that aims to improve a users overall mental and physical wellbeing by cmaking positive changes to their daily habits and routines. The app will be a resource that will promote healthy eating, sleep advice, and improve mental and physical health through stress reduction methods and techniques. By using the app users will change their habits and thought processes for one day which could lead to the development of new life changing habits.

The mobile app was created following iOS guidelines for iPhone as well as a material design version for Android.

The main tools used for this project were:

  • Balsamiq.
  • Sketch.
  • Invision.

Objective

There are a number of related apps on the market currently that provide similar content however my app will be different to these apps as it will encompass elements of both mental and physical health within the one app while also adopting a more simplified approach focusing on each part of a users day that they can adjust to bring about better health.

Content will be provided for a morning routine, daily commute and evening routine to encourage new habits that promote both mental and physical wellbeing.

Key features proposed:

  • Morning Routine Section - Be able to choose from breakfast suggestions, stretching exercises, meditation, view helpful tips and suggestions.
  • Commuting Section - Be able to listen to podcasts, listen to relaxing music, view relaxing imagery and motivational content.
  • Evening Routine Section - Be able to view content on sleep, meditation, exercise, outdoor activities, healthy evening meals, tips to unwind.
  • User Profile Section - Allow a user to set up a profile to view their favourite content from their activity on the app.

02

User Flows

The requirement for the project was to design two versions for the mobile app, one for iOS and one for Android. The brief stated to create 3 user flows that detail how users will complete particular tasks in the app, taking care not to make the app too complicated as there will be two verisons designed.

User Story 1

"As a user I want to be able to view images and instructions of a morning stretching routine"

User Story 2

“As a user I want to be able to view beautiful images of nature when I am commuting to work”

User Story 3

“As a user I want to be able to listen to a sleepcast before I go to bed”

03

Wireframes

The user flows formed the foundation for initial low fidelity wireframes using balsamiq. These initial wireframes were designed for iOS bearing in mind iOS guidelines. At this stage sketching using paper and pencil would have also been appropriate but because I had not used the Balsamiq software yet I wanted to use this low fidelity wireframing approach. The screens developed gave me an initial idea as to how the elements would be placed and what content would be included in the app as well as considerations such as the navigation elements - footer menu , tabs, user search form etc.

High Fidelity Wireframes

After initital wireframes were developed a further phase involved researching similar apps on the web to determine features and elements that could be incorporated into my mobile app also. Also during this phase of iteration I discussed my designs with my tutor and based on feedback as well as guidelines researched for iOS and Android a further iteration of high fidelity wireframes were developed.

Careful consideration was paid to the end users of the app during the visual design process. Areas of design that were researched during this phase included; Colour theory and principles, UI trends, Typography, Flat Design & Material Design and Iconography.

The idea for the design was to use different colours for the different sections within the app - home, morning, commuting and evening. It makes sense to use warm colours such as yellow and orange for the morning to promote the feeling of energy and freshness aligned with the sun, and the evening uses a relaxing darker colour in this case purple and blue, which have associations with spirituality and calmness. A card UI design is used within the app with imagery representing the content that the user can link to.

04

Usability Testing

The next phase was to create a prototype that could be tested with users for feedback. Based on this feedback changes were made to the designs before being finalized. The overall feedback from users that tested the app was positive. They liked the idea to use colours to identify different moments of the day. They felt that the screens were well designed and that the styling worked well with the theme, making them feel relaxed. They also felt that the intention of the app was clear and that they knew how to use it from the screens presented. Some constructive critism lead to some design changes as outlined below.

One pain point for users of the app seemed to be the 'Commuting' screen. They felt that the tabs at the top of the screen looked wrong and they would have preferred the screen design to have the same style as the 'Morning' screen, with cards used for the different selection choices. This feedback was taken on board and the screen was redesigned as can be seen below.

Before Testing

After Testing

Another piece of feedback related to the 'Sleepcast' screen, where users felt that there was too much horizontal spacing between the card elements. The padding between the cards was corrected for consistency and improves the visual design.

Before Testing

After Testing

For the login and sign up screens there was some useful feedback from users. They noted that the sign up button blended into the background image so was not clear to see, and as this is a call to action button it should contrast with the background to be easily identified. The solution that was used was to lower the opacity of the background image and use a white input box behind the login and sign up fields so that they stood out more. Also changing the colour of the sign up button from dark grey to orange so that it more visible. Another piece of feedback was that the screen looked a bit bare, so the logo design was incorporated into these screens also.

Final Wireframes

Based on the feedback from the usability testing the design was modified and the final screens were developed. The changes inlcuded the issues as already mentioned where the spacing and alignment was corrected on screens and the login and sign up screens were adjusted. In addition the tabs for Android were changed to be more in line with material design, where an underline under text is used as opposed to a rounded button style. Additional screens were also developed in the final design to include an error message screen if a user enters their login details incorrectly, and a tooltip for the sign up screen that shows a user if they have entered a strong password during sign up. Also an empty screen for when a user enters an invalid search choice was created to demonstrate how this feedback would appear to the user.

06

Retrospective

What went well?

As this was my third project using the Sketch software I am more comforatable and quicker using it which has saved time during the wireframes iteration phases. Early research into existing apps that I can learn from as well as studying UI elelments that related to iOS and Android also helped in the desing of the wireframes. The usability tests also yielded some constructive feedback that I was able to use to improve the final screens.

Skills:

Wireframes, Usability Testing.

Processes:

User centered design,

Learnings:

Research design sites and guidelines online, Engage with end users for design feedback.

What was a challenge?

This is the first time that I designed screens for Android so becoming aware of the design guidelines used for material design was a learning curve. Some of the early screens I designed had to be created again as they did not fit the material design recommendations.

Skills:

Material Design.

Processes:

User centered design.

Learnings:

Research native guidelines for iOS & Android further.
