Falcon Airlines

Print

The Brief

From studying historic and contemporary design practitioners, the task was to complete a finished piece of work in the form of an A3 poster using one of the design techniques researched. The design should be based on images or locations that were encountered on a journey to work.

Concept Development

A comparative analysis previously undertaken focused on Swiss style graphic design and this was the style that was developed for this project. An initial idea was to use the subject of flight as the theme for the piece. Swiss style design was researched including posters created by designers such as Armin Hofmann, Max Huber and Joseph Müller-Brockmann. Initial sketches were developed along with experiments using different mediums and a mood board was also created.

The design uses negative space to create the image of a falcon. An airplane wing is used at the bottom part of the poster to give the poster depth, and two identical shapes that represent a cockpit window to the right of the poster also form the tail for the falcon image. A corporate blue is used for the airplane shapes as blue symbolizes trust, loyalty, confidence and intelligence and is also associated with stability.These are all appropriate themes for an airline. Blue also promotes a feeling of calmness and wellbeing. A light blue triangular shape representing the sky and freedom helps enforce the overall theme and promotes the feeling of movement with the sky in the background. A red colour is added as it is assocaited with energy and power to give the piece a dynamic effect. The type used in the poster is ‘Akzidenz Grotesk’ as this was a favourite typeface of the Swiss practitioners that I had researched.
