Microsoft's New Commerce Experience

Microsoft's evolution to the Cloud Subscription Program, enforcing changes to purchasing, cancellations, upgrades and renewals for 365 subscriptions.

My role: UX Product Designer

The Challenge

In January 2022 Microsoft introduced an enhanced version of it's CSP program. The changes were enforced by Microsoft to all it's partners, which meant that these changes had to be adopted by SoftwareONE. Changes impacted the partner portal 'ONEClub' in terms of how partners purchased, cancelled and managed their software subscriptions. Being the UX Product Designer for ONEClub it was my responsibility to work with the business and partners to re-design the portal in order to introduce these changes while providing an enhanced user experience.

This project involved numerous meetings and discussions with key stakeholders and development teams working to a time sensitive deadline. The project was undertaken from February-July 2022, implemented in stages, in conjunction with the various daily responsibilities and workload of my role.

The skills I used for this project included:

Qualitative Research, Quantitative Research, Usability Testing, Interaction Design, User Interface Design, Data Analysis, Communication & Soft Skills.

My Design Process

Interviews

Qualitative Research

Stakeholder Meetings

I attended a number of early fact finding meetings with key stakeholders within the business in order to determine what the requirements for the proejct would be for UX. I met with the business owner of the product as well as the PM and also Business Development Managers. During weekly planning meetings for the product development teams were also involved in the discussions. It became clear that delivering on the project would be the top priority for the team. The requirements were discussed and it was decided that the implementations would be carried out in stages according to priority and timelines.

The enhanced functionality that would need to be designed, tested and implemented in the portal included:

  1. A new additional purchasing term and billing option.
  2. Changes to rules regarding cancellation of subscriptions.
  3. Changes to the rules for upsizing and downsizing of subscriptions.
  4. New auto-renewal functionality.
  5. New functionality for mid-term upgrading of subscriptions.
  6. New functionality for scheduling changes to subscriptions at renewal end-date.
  7. New functionality to track subscription quantities that could be reduced within certain time frames.
  8. A self serve migration tool was also needed to allow partners to migrate their subscriptions from legacy to the New Commerce subscription offering.

Customer Preview Calls

I also spoke to partners during monthly recurring customer preview calls. The feedback from partners was that they wanted the new functionality being introduced by Microsoft and they felt that their customers would also demand these enhancements. Speaking to development managers from within the business they gave me the same feedback from customers. So both the business and our customers were aligned that these changes were needed and a top priority.

Persona: IT Leader

The main persona for this project is the CIO or the IT Leader. His focus is on remaining competitive, and improving operational efficiency and his ROI.

He has a strong technical background, often starting as an IT Admin or developer and working his way up the ladder. He reports on cloud and IT Spend to the c-level.

Problem Statements

Ideate & Prototype

Purchasing of Subscriptions

As part of the New Commercial Experience Microsoft introduced an additional purchasing option that had to be brought into the portal. Up to this point there were two purchasing options 1) Monthly Billing with a Monthly Commitmentand 2) Yearly Billing with an Annual Commitment. The New Commerce Experience added a third option - an Annual Commitment Term with a Monthly Billing option. Reviewing the portal it became clear that the purchasing cards in use would not be suitable for this additional third buying option. This lead to a re-design of the product list and product detail pages.

Legacy Product List

The existing product card design showed two purchasing options on the card in the product list view. The user could pick from one of these options and then add to cart immediately. This design could not be scaled to include a third buying option so a re-design of the product card was needed.

Screenshot of Legacy Product List cards in the portal

New Commerce Product List

An early approach was to to introduce the thrid buying option onto the product card in the product list. This design was tested but failed the UAT with users, as it was felt to be overly complex and confusing. The legacy design was not scalable and so a new approach was needed.

Mockup of design iteration for the New Commerce product list cards

Design Iteration

Final Design

The design was rejected and following on from further iterations and research a final design was proposed. One purchasing option would be shown on the product card and the 'Buy Now' button would be changed to a 'View all buying options', the user flow would now change to go into the product card detail where the user could choose a buying option and then move into the checkout flow. Consulting with the business it was decided that the monthly pricing option would be shown as the default as it was the cheapest option and it was the industry standard for displaying e-commerce pricing terms.

Mockup of the final design for the New Commerce product list cards

Legacy Product Detail

In the existing product detail page the user chooses the billing cycle from a dropdown menu. The billing cycle would be either monthly or annual billing. The price shown would be updated to reflect this.

Screenshot of Legacy Product Detail card in the portal

New Commerce Product Detail

The thrid buying option was introduced into the product detail card. This lead to a complete redesign of this page. The user journey would now take the user from the product list into the product detail where all three buying options would be displayed. Initially the purchasing offerings were grouped by the commitment term, being either monthly or annual. The additional billing options were then displayed. This approach was tested with users in the business and was felt to be too complex, so was not chosen as a final design proposal.

Mockup of design iteration for the New Commerce product detail card

Design Iteration

Final Design

In a refined design the commitment term is included in each offering and not grouped as before. Another row was also added to the cards to show particular information for that offering such as savings or flexibility. As a default the monthly commitment and billing offering is highlighted but a savings badge was introduced for the yearly billing and annual commitment offering to show the user the savings they could make if they purchased this option.

Mockup of the final design for the New Commerce product detail card

Data Analysis

Funnel: Cart > Checkout Analysis

It was important to analyse user adoption of the new buying cards. Heap Analytics was used for this data analysis. A new event was defined in Heap and used to create a separate Cart > Checout funnel for New Commerce products. The intention was to validate the new design and to also obtain Quantitative data that backed up the Qualitative feedback from users during the UAT sessions of the new purchasing cards. The analysis was run for a 3 month period, following on from the deployment of the new cards to production in March 2022.

Legacy Card

Funnel: Cart > Checkout (March 1st - May 31st 2022)

The legacy card funnel had a conversion rate of 80%, with the highest drop-off happening in step1 - the add to cart step. Some users were adding to cart from the product list card but then not moving into the shopping cart to complete the buying journey.

New Commerce Card

Funnel: Cart > Checkout (March 1st - May 31st 2022)

The New Commerce card funnel had a conversion rate of 93.4% for the same period analysed. This was an increased conversion of 13.4% when compared to the Legacy product card design. The highest drop-off for New Commerce was happening in step2 - the shopping cart page. Some users were moving into the cart but they were not continuing to the checkout to complete the buying journey. A separate piece of analysis was undertaken to determine reasons for user abandonment in step2 of the funnel. However the overall conversion rate was very positive and validated the new design.

Note: The volume of users shown in the New Commerce funnel is lower than in the Legacy card funnel as more products were still using the legacy design - only 365 products moved to New Commerce.

Managing Subscriptions

The new commerce experience extended the functionality for managing purchased subscriptions also. Some of the changes that had to be brought into the ONEClub portal included:

  1. Auto-renewal functionality.
  2. Upgrade functionality for an immediate upgrade on a subscription mid-term before renewal date.
  3. Ability to schedule changes to the subscription to take effect at the subscription renewal date.
  4. A way of tracking the time allowed for reducing subscription quantities inline with Microsoft riles.
  5. A tool to allow partners to migrate their subscriptions from Legacy to New Commerce .

1 - Auto-Renewal Toggle

With the new experience Microsoft enforced stricter rules relating to cancellations. After 7 days of purchase a subscription cound not longer be cancelled until the subscription renewal, where a 7 day period would be available again. Additional functionality was introduced that allowed partners to disable subscription renewal. This was introduced to the subscription detail screen by using a renewal toggle switch. In addition a tooltip was used to inform partners of the business rules if the toggle is switched on or off.

2.1 - Upgrades (Subscription Detail)

Another important change that Microsoft introduced was the abililty to provide mid-term upgrades options to higher products. Partial and full upgrades were possible, including the option of changing commitment term and billing cycle. This offering had to be designed and implemented into the portal.

A number of designs were tested with participants from within the company and this lead to the final proposed design that was assigned to the development team. The initial designs were created and tested using Sketch. During the UAT of the design a number of other improvements were made to the subscription detail page including introducing a commitment term onto the detail page and updating the cancellation button.

Iteration1

Iteration2

Iteration3

The label was changed on the primary button to 'View Upgrade Options' as users felt that 'Upgrade Now' would automatically perform an upgrade action. This button actually opens a side panel of possible upgrades for this subscription, and the upgrade is performed later in the user flow. The placement of the button was also moved to be above the quantity increase/decrease function and some padding was introduced as upgrades are a separate action and not related to this function, so should not be grouped together.

2.2 - Upgrades (User Flow)
