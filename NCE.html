Microsoft's New Commerce Experience

Microsoft's evolution to the Cloud Subscription Program, enforcing changes to purchasing, cancellations, upgrades and renewals for 365 subscriptions.

My role: UX Product Designer

The Challenge

In January 2022 Microsoft introduced an enhanced version of it's CSP program. The changes were enforced by Microsoft to all it's partners, which meant that these changes had to be adopted by SoftwareONE. Changes impacted the partner portal 'ONEClub' in terms of how partners purchased, cancelled and managed their software subscriptions. Being the UX Product Designer for ONEClub it was my responsibility to work with the business and partners to re-design the portal in order to introduce these changes while providing an enhanced user experience.

This project involved numerous meetings and discussions with key stakeholders and development teams working to a time sensitive deadline. The project was undertaken from February-July 2022, implemented in stages, in conjunction with the various daily responsibilities and workload of my role.

The skills I used for this project included:

Qualitative Research, Quantitative Research, Usability Testing, Interaction Design, User Interface Design, Data Analysis, Communication & Soft Skills.

Problem Statements

Ideate & Prototype

Purchasing of Subscriptions

As part of the New Commercial Experience Microsoft introduced an additional purchasing option that had to be brought into the portal. Up to this point there were two purchasing options 1) Monthly Billing with a Monthly Commitmentand 2) Yearly Billing with an Annual Commitment. The New Commerce Experience added a third option - an Annual Commitment Term with a Monthly Billing option. Reviewing the portal it became clear that the purchasing cards in use would not be suitable for this additional third buying option. This lead to a re-design of the product list and product detail pages.

Legacy Product List

The existing product card design showed two purchasing options on the card in the product list view. The user could pick from one of these options and then add to cart immediately. This design could not be scaled to include a third buying option so a re-design of the product card was needed.

Paper Prototyping

I like to start the prototyping stage by creating paper prototypes until I think I have some designs that could fit the problem. This can be helpful to ideate in this form initially in order to gain some clarity of the user flow and also how the UI would need to be changed. It can save time in the long run as it is easier to redraw a paper prototype than to change a high fidelity mockup.

Once I had some ideas I then created high fidelity mockups using Sketch, that I could then test with users to gain some feedback from them.

New Commerce Product List

An early approach was to to introduce the thrid buying option onto the product card in the product list. This design was tested but failed the UAT with users, as it was felt to be overly complex and confusing.

This legacy design was not scalable and so a new approach was needed.

A better approach was to use one purchasing option on the product card where the 'Buy Now' button would be changed to 'View all buying options', the user flow would now change to go into the product card detail where the user could choose a buying option and then move into the checkout flow.

Legacy Product Detail

In the existing product detail page the user chooses the billing cycle from a dropdown menu. The billing cycle would be either monthly or annual billing. The price shown would be updated to reflect this.

New Commerce Product Detail

The third buying option was introduced into the product detail card. This lead to a complete re-design of this page. Initially the purchasing offerings were grouped by the commitment term, being either monthly or annual. The additional billing options were then displayed. This approach was tested with users in the business and was felt to be too complex, so was not chosen as a final design proposal.

In a refined design the commitment term is included in each offering and not grouped as before. Another row was also added to the cards to show particular information for that offering such as savings or flexibility. As a default the monthly commitment and billing offering is highlighted but a savings badge was introduced for the yearly billing and annual commitment offering.

Feature Adoption - Analysis

Funnel: Cart > Checkout Analysis

It was important to analyse user adoption of the new buying cards. Heap Analytics was used for this data analysis. A new event was defined in Heap and used to create a separate Cart > Checout funnel for New Commerce products. The intention was to validate the new design and to also obtain Quantitative data that backed up the Qualitative feedback from users during the UAT sessions of the new purchasing cards. The analysis was run for a 6 month period, following on from the deployment of the new cards to production in March 2022.

Legacy Card

Funnel: Cart > Checkout (March 1st - Aug 31st 2022)

The legacy card funnel had a conversion rate of 84%, with the highest drop-off happening in step 1 - the add to cart step. Some users were adding to cart from the product list card but then not moving into the shopping cart to complete the buying journey. A separate piece of analysis and design was undertaken to re-design the mini-cart which can be viewed {LINK TO CART UX IMPROVEMENT} in my portfolio.

New Commerce Card

Funnel: Cart > Checkout (March 1st - Aug 31st 2022)

The New Commerce card funnel had a conversion rate of 94.8% for the same period analysed. This was an increased conversion of 10.8% when compared to the Legacy product card design. The highest drop-off for New Commerce was happening in step 2 - the shopping cart page. Some users were moving into the cart but they were not continuing to the checkout to complete the buying journey. A separate piece of analysis was undertaken to determine reasons for user abandonment in step 2 of the funnel. However the overall conversion rate was very positive and validated the new design.

Note: The volume of users shown in the New Commerce funnel is lower than in the Legacy card funnel as more products were still using the legacy design - only Microsoft 365 products moved to New Commerce.

Managing Subscriptions

The new commerce experience extended the functionality for managing purchased subscriptions also. Some of the changes that had to be brought into the ONEClub portal included:

  1. Auto-renewal functionality.
  2. Upgrade functionality for an immediate upgrade on a subscription mid-term before renewal date.
  3. Ability to schedule changes to the subscription to take effect at the subscription renewal date.
  4. A way of tracking the time allowed for reducing subscription quantities inline with Microsoft rules.

1 - Auto-Renewal Toggle

Additional functionality was introduced that allowed partners to disable subscription renewal. This was introduced to the subscription detail screen by using a renewal toggle switch. In addition a tooltip was used to inform partners of the business rules if the toggle is switched on or off.

2.1 - Upgrades (Planning)

Another important change that Microsoft introduced was the abililty to provide mid-term upgrades options to higher products. Partial and full upgrades were possible, including the option of changing commitment term and billing cycle. This offering had to be designed and implemented into the portal.

2.2 - Upgrades (Subscription Detail)

Another important change that Microsoft introduced was the abililty to provide mid-term upgrades options to higher products. Partial and full upgrades were possible, including the option of changing commitment term and billing cycle. This offering had to be designed and implemented into the portal.

Sketch prototypes were created and a number of designs were tested with participants from within the company. In total I conducted 9 remote moderated usability tests and this lead to the final proposed design that was assigned to the development team. During the UAT of the design a number of other improvements were made to the subscription detail page including introducing a commitment term onto the detail page and updating the cancellation button.

Improvements to the Design:

  • Usability: The label was changed on the primary button to 'View Upgrade Options' as users felt that 'Upgrade Now' would automatically perform an upgrade action. This button actually opens a side panel of possible upgrades for this subscription, and the upgrade is performed later in the user flow.
  • Proximity: The placement of the upgrade button was also moved to be above the quantity increase/decrease function and some padding was introduced as upgrades are a separate action and not related to this function, so should not be grouped together. Some users were going to change quantities before upgrading.
  • Visual Weight: The design and placement of the cancel subscription button was updated as this action was not clear enough to users.

Issues to Consider:

  • He was wondering if he could upgrade with an increase in quantity as well? Or how would he know if he could do that?
  • Would the upgrade button only appear when there is an upgrade option?
  • Need a message before the user upgrades to let them know of the new price.
  • He didn’t notice the confirmation message when the subscription had been upgraded.
  • He didn’t see the link in the confirmation notification that links to the license that was upgraded.

2.3 - Upgrades (Side Panel Component)

A side panel component was then designed that would open from the right of the screen when the user clicked on the 'View Upgrade Options' primary button. I researched how other Ecommerce websites were using a side panel and also read documentation available on material.io. I created a new component for the design system based on this and also worked with the UI Designer to create guidelines for its use, so that other designers in the team could reference this.

4.1 Checking License Reductions (Subscription Detail)

Another change to licensing that was imposed as part of NCE was the time period allowed for reduction of subscription quantities. The new business rules meant that there was a 7 day window where the partner could reduce quantities of the subscription. Also whenever the partner increased the quantity of a subscription they received an additional 7 day window where they could make a reduction to that specific quantity if needed. Outside of this they would have to wait until the subscription renewed again before they would be able to reduce the quantity or alternatively they could use the new tool introduced to schedule a change to the quantity of the subscription at renewal.

Partners needed a way of being able to track how many licenses attached to a subscription could be reduced, and what the time frames were for these possible reductions. This additional functionality was also designed , tested and deployed in the portal.

4.3 - Gestalt Principles of Design

I followed the principles of gestalt theory of visual perception to guide my design. I considered visual hierarchy, similarity, proximity, visual weight, symmetry and order.

Implement

Testing with the development team

Testing the functionality in development

Before I could schedule UAT tests with users to test the usability of the new commerce features I needed to ensure that the complex business logic and functionality were correct. I scheduled calls with the front-end QA and development team to test the user journeys. During the testing we discovered a number of issues such as back-end dependencies with the data not updating correctly following on from a task such as an upgrade or adjustment of subscription quantity. Also there were front-end corrections that needed to be made where the logic of the implementation or the UI of the features was not correct.

Once these issues were corrected I again reviewed the implementation with the team and then gave the go-ahead for the partial release into produciton on a test account. It was important to test again in production using a test account to ensure that correct pricing was being applied, there were limitations in development where dummy pricing data was being used.
