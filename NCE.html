Microsoft's New Commerce Experience

Microsoft's evolution to the Cloud Subscription Program, enforcing changes to purchasing, cancellations, upgrades and renewals for 365 subscriptions.

My role: UX Product Designer

The Challenge

In January 2022 Microsoft introduced an enhanced version of it's CSP program. The changes were enforced by Microsoft to all it's partners, which meant that these changes had to be adopted by SoftwareONE. Changes impacted the partner portal 'ONEClub' in terms of how partners purchased, cancelled and managed their software subscriptions for 365 products, namely Office 365, Microsoft 365, Windows 365 and Dynamics 365. Being the UX Product Designer for ONEClub it was my responsibility to work with the business and partners to re-design the portal in order to introduce these changes while providing an enhanced user experience.

This project involved numerous meetings and discussions with key stakeholders and development teams working to a time sensitive deadline. The project was undertaken from February-October 2022, implemented in stages, in conjunction with the various daily responsibilities and workload of my role. The first part of the project involved re-designing the existing buying cards to introduce a third purchasing option. Later stages involved adding additional functionality to the subscription detail and list view to include the new commerce model.

The skills I used for this project included:

Qualitative Research, Quantitative Research, Usability Testing, Interaction Design, User Interface Design, Data Analysis, Communication & Soft Skills.

My Design Process

I met with the business owner of the product as well as the PM and also Business Development Managers. During weekly planning meetings for the product development teams were also involved in the discussions. It became clear that delivering on the project would be the top priority for the team. I also spoke to partners during monthly recurring customer preview calls. The feedback from partners was that they wanted the new functionality being introduced by Microsoft and they felt that their customers would also demand these enhancements. Speaking to stakeholders from within the business they gave me the same feedback from customers. So both the business and our customers were aligned that these changes were needed and a top priority.

Define

Persona: Sales Manager

I reviewed the qualitative data gathered from speaking to partners in customer preview calls and identified their Goals, Frustrations and Needs. I then grouped these into post-it notes on a Miro board. This led to the creation of the the 'Sales Manager' persona.

He is an expert in licensing and has strong technical knowledge having over 10 years expereince working in licensing. He works for a small company of 10-50 employees. His focus is on meeting his customers needs, staying updated on industry developments, efficient time management and driving business growth. He wants to accomplish his job functions as quickly as posssible when using the portal, buying or upsizing licensing for customers or running billing reports. He is frustrated by poor backend performance, where he has to wait on pages to load or by missing or inaccurate data on the portal.

Other challenges he faces are the complex and changing business logic and rules that he must be aware of as he is a Microsoft reseller.

Ideate & Prototype

Purchasing of Subscriptions

As part of the New Commercial Experience Microsoft introduced an additional purchasing option that had to be brought into the portal. Up to this point there were two purchasing options 1) Annual Commitment with Annual Billing and 2) Annual Commitment with Monthly Billing cycle. The New Commerce Experience added a third option - a Monthly Commitment Term with a Monthly Billing cycle. Reviewing the portal it became clear that the purchasing cards in use would not be suitable for this additional third buying option. This lead to a re-design of the product list and product detail pages.

Legacy Product List

The existing product card design showed two purchasing options on the card in the product list view. The user could pick from one of these options and then add to cart immediately. This design could not be scaled to include a third buying option so a re-design of the product card was needed.

Paper Prototyping

I like to start the prototyping stage by creating paper prototypes until I think I have some designs that could fit the problem. This can be helpful to ideate in this form initially in order to gain some clarity of the user flow and also how the UI would need to be changed. It can save time in the long run as it is easier to redraw a paper prototype than to change a high fidelity mockup.

Once I had some ideas I then created high fidelity mockups using Sketch, that I could then test with users to gain some feedback from them.

New Commerce Product List

An early approach was to to introduce the thrid buying option onto the product card in the product list. This design was tested with users, but it was felt to be overly complex and confusing.

This legacy design was not scalable and so a new approach was needed.

A re-designed approach was to use one purchasing option on the product card where the 'Buy Now' button would be changed to 'View all buying options', the user flow would now change to go into the product card detail where the user could choose a buying option and then move into the checkout flow.

Legacy Product Detail

In the existing product detail page the user chooses the billing cycle from a dropdown menu. The billing cycle would be either monthly or annual billing. The price shown would be updated to reflect this.

New Commerce Product Detail

The third buying option was introduced into the product detail card. This lead to a complete re-design of this page. Initially the purchasing offerings were grouped by the commitment term, being either monthly or annual. The additional billing options were then displayed. This approach was tested with users in the business and was felt to be too complex, so was not chosen as a final design proposal.

In a refined design the commitment term is included in each offering and not grouped as before. Another row was also added to the cards to show particular information for that offering such as savings or flexibility. As a default the monthly commitment and billing offering is highlighted but a savings badge was introduced for the yearly billing and annual commitment offering.

Feature Adoption - Analysis

Funnel: Product > Shopping Cart Analysis

It was important to analyse user adoption of the new buying cards in the product list/catalogue as well as in the product detail. Heap Analytics was used for this data analysis. New events were defined in Heap and used to count engagement with the new NCE product cards in the product list page. The clicks on the 'View all buying options' button on the NCE card were obtained. When the user clicks on this button they are taken to the product detail page. The engagement on the new NCE product options were defined in an event along with the clicks on the 'Buy Now' or 'Add to Cart' buttons on this page. The conversion was measured between these steps and from there the conversion to the shopping cart page was also included.

The intention was to validate the new design and to also obtain quantitative data that backed up the qualitative feedback from users during the UAT sessions of the new purchasing cards. The analysis was run for a 6 month period, following on from the deployment of the new cards to production in March 2022.

Funnel: NCE Product > Shopping Cart (March 1st - Aug 31st 2022)

The overall conversion rate from users clicking on the NCE product in the product list to adding the item and moving to the checkout page was 81.2% for the period analysed. The median time engaged with the buying option cards in the product detail was 58 seconds. So it was taking users less than a minute to find the offering they needed before deciding to buy and move to the shopping cart page.

The highest drop-off for was happening in step 1 - the product detail page. Some users were moving into the product detail but they were not continuing to the checkout to complete the buying journey. A separate piece of analysis was undertaken to determine reasons for user abandonment in step 1 of the funnel. It was determined that this abandonment could be users searching for a different product, adding to cart but not moving to the cart or being logged off the portal.

The steps taken to improve the drop off from users included a re-design of the product catalogue (view project) so that products listed would be based on partners purchasing history, thus helping them find products they needed. The mini-cart was also re-designed to introduce common design patters (view project), and the inactive log off time in the portal was extended from 5 minutes to 15 minutes.

However the overall conversion rate was quite positive and validated the new design.

Managing Subscriptions

The new commerce experience extended the functionality for managing purchased subscriptions also. Some of the changes that had to be brought into the ONEClub portal included:

  1. Auto-renewal functionality.
  2. Upgrade functionality for an immediate upgrade on a subscription mid-term before renewal date.
  3. Ability to schedule changes to the subscription to take effect at the subscription renewal date.
  4. A way of tracking the time allowed for reducing subscription quantities inline with Microsoft rules.

1 - Auto-Renewal Toggle

Additional functionality was introduced that allowed partners to disable subscription renewal. This was introduced to the subscription detail screen by using a renewal toggle switch. In addition a tooltip was used to inform partners of the business rules if the toggle is switched on or off.

2.1 - Upgrades (Planning)

Another important change that Microsoft introduced was the abililty to provide mid-term upgrades options to higher products. Partial and full upgrades were possible, including the option of changing commitment term and billing cycle. This offering had to be designed and implemented into the portal.

2.2 - Upgrades (Subscription Detail)

Another important change that Microsoft introduced was the abililty to provide mid-term upgrade options to higher products. Partial and full upgrades were possible, including the option of changing commitment term and billing cycle. This offering had to be designed and implemented into the portal.

Sketch prototypes were created and a number of designs were tested with participants from within the company. In total I conducted 9 remote moderated usability tests and this lead to the final proposed design that was assigned to the development team. During the UAT of the design a number of other improvements were made to the subscription detail page including introducing a commitment term onto the detail page and updating the cancellation button.

Improvements to the Design:

  • Usability: The label was changed on the primary button to 'View Upgrade Options' as users felt that 'Upgrade Now' would automatically perform an upgrade action. This button actually opens a side panel of possible upgrades for this subscription, and the upgrade is performed later in the user flow.
  • Proximity: The placement of the upgrade button was also moved to be above the quantity increase/decrease function and some padding was introduced as upgrades are a separate action and not related to this function, so should not be grouped together. Some users were going to change quantities before upgrading.
  • Visual Weight: The design and placement of the cancel subscription button was updated as this action was not clear enough to users.

Issues to Improve:

  • Users were wondering if they could upgrade with an increase in quantity as well? Or how would they know if they could do that?
  • Would the upgrade button only appear when there is an upgrade option available?
  • Design needs a message before the user upgrades to let them know of the new price.
  • They didn’t notice the confirmation message when the subscription had been upgraded.
  • They didn’t see the link in the confirmation notification that links to the license that was upgraded.

2.3 - Upgrades (Side Panel Component)

A side panel component was then designed that would open from the right of the screen when the user clicked on the 'View Upgrade Options' primary button. I researched how other Ecommerce websites were using a side panel and also read documentation available on material.io. I created a new component for the design system based on this and also worked with the UI Designer to create guidelines for its use, so that other designers in the team could reference this.

3.1 Scheduling Renewals (Subscription Detail)

With the new experience from Microsoft partners would now also be able to schedule changes to their subscriptions that would take effect when the subscription renews. This was an important addition to add to the portal as it provided the ability to schedule a downgrade to a subscription at the end of subscription term.

3.2 - Scheduling Renewals (Side Panel Component)

The same side panel component was used previously for upgrades was used, that would open from the right of the screen when the user clicked on the 'Schedule Renewal Changes' secondary button. There was a change to the copy shown to the partner to advise them of this additional functionality. The same dropdown components were used in the design, the main difference being the API calls from the backend database would be changed as this feature is distinct from the upgrades tool.

4 - License Reductions

Another change to licensing that was imposed as part of NCE was the time period allowed for reduction of subscription quantities. The new business rules meant that there was a 7 day window where the partner could reduce the quantities of the subscription. Also whenever the partner increased the quantity of a subscription they received an additional 7 day window where they could make a reduction to that specific quantity if needed. Outside of this they would have to wait until the subscription renewed again before they would be able to reduce the quantity or alternatively they could use the new scheduling tool to schedule a change to the quantity of the subscription at the renewal date.

A side panel component was used again, that would open from the right of the screen whenever the user tried to downsize by a quantity that was more than was allowed under the Microsoft 7 day rule. A table was shown to dispaly the total amount of licenses that were available to downsize immediately and the respective time frames allowed.

Gestalt Principles of Design

I followed the principles of gestalt theory of visual perception to guide my design for this project. I considered visual hierarchy, similarity, proximity, visual weight as well as symmetry and order.

Side Panel Component - Design System

I also created a new side panel component for use in the team design system. The other members of the team worked on a separate product so the component that was created used this other products brand colour for button components. I included guidelines for best use of the side panel based on my online research. I included the anatomy of the side panel with descriptions of each element and also a best practice section for usability and a section on accessibility (content contrast, keyboard focus of buttons and input elements and text contrast).

Implement

Testing with the development team

I scheduled calls with the front-end QA and development team to test that the complex business logic and functionality were correct. During the testing we discovered a number of issues such as back-end dependencies with the data not updating correctly. Also there were front-end corrections that needed to be made where the logic of the implementation or the UI of the features was not correct.

Once these issues were corrected I again reviewed the implementation with the team and then gave the go-ahead for the partial release into produciton on a test account to ensure that correct pricing was being applied, as there were limitations in development where dummy pricing data was being used.

UAT Reports - Usability Testing

Statistical Charts

As part of the defined Release Management process I completed the UAT (user acceptance testing) for the Upgrades, Schedule Renewals and License Reduction tools. I scheduled UAT's with 5 internal users and asked each participant to complete some simple tasks. The users had a mix of Low, Mid and High expertise levels. I graded each test with a fail (did not complete the task) or success (completed the task). The feedback from these UAT's was recorded in a dataset and a script created in 'R' was run to complile the data and create the charts displayed here in this section. The graph shows the rate of pass and failure for all users for the latest iteration of the design.

1) Upgrades Tool

All users were able to complete all tasks successfully, resulting in a 100% task completion rate. They were able to locate the upgrade button on the interface and were able to choose a subscription, quantity to upgrade, commitment term and billing cycle. They were able to locate the new subscription that was upgraded and validate the details were correct. Some feedback from users were that the performance was a bit slow when the side panel was being opened - they had to wait and view a 'spinner wheel' as an API was being called from the database. I brought this to the attention of the backend database team to try to improve the performance here to reduce any user frustration.

2) Scheduling Renewals Tool

There was one minor fail (16.67%) recorded during the tests on one task only - the discoverability of the tool. One user was not able to locate the correct button when asked to perform the task to schedule a change. This task still passed as all other participants (83.33%) were able to find this tool correctly. Some feedback was also recorded about possible future enhancements to the tool that would make it more usable. This related to beign able to modify a saved change where there was a backend restriction. I brought this feedback to the database team to look for improvements in this area.

3) License Reductions Tool

All users were able to complete all tasks successfully, resulting in a 100% task completion rate. They were abe to understand what was happening and were able to view the number of quantities that were available to reduce immediately and the time allowed for each, and were able to make the needed change. I asked them to rate the usability of the tool and also collected some of their feedback for future improvements to the design.
