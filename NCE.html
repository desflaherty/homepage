New Commerce Experience

Microsoft's evolution to the Cloud Subscription Program, enforcing changes to purchasing, cancellations, upgrades and renewals for 365 subscriptions.

My role: UX Product Designer

TL;DR

In Janurary 2022 Microsoft introduced an enhanced version of it's CSP program. The changes were enforced by Microsoft to all it's partners, which meant they had to be adopted by SoftwareONE. Changes impacted the partner portal 'ONEClub' in terms of how partners purchased, cancelled and managed their software subscriptions. Being the UX Product Designer for ONEClub it was my responsibility to work with the business and to re-design the portal to introduce these changes while providing an enhanced experience. The project was undertaken from February-June 2022, in conjunction with the various daily responsibilities in my role.

Purchasing of subscriptions

As part of the New Commercial Experience Microsoft introduced an additional purchasing option that had to be brought into the portal. Up to this point there were two purchasing options 1) Monthly Billing with a Monthly Commitment and 2) Yearly Billing with an Annual Commitment. The New Expereince added a third option - an Annual Commitment but with Monthly Billing. Reviewing the portal it became clear that the purchasing cards in use would not be suitable for this additional third buying option. This lead to a redesign of the product list and product detail pages.

Legacy Product List

The existing product card design showed two purchasing options on the card in the product list view. The user could pick from one of these options and then add to cart immediately. This design could not be scaled to include a third buying option so a redesign of the product card was needed.

New Commerce Product List

An early approach was to to intorduce the thrid buying option onto the product card in the product list. This design was tested but failed the UAT with users, as it was felt to be overly complex and confusing. The legacy design was not scalable and so a new approach was needed.

Design Iteration

Final Design

The design was rejected and following on from further iterations and research a final design was proposed. One purchasing option would be shown on the product card and the 'buy now' button would be changed to a 'view all buying options', the user flow would now change to go into the product detail card where the user could choose a buying option and then move into the checkout flow. Consulting with the business it was decided that the monthly pricing option would be shown as the default as it was the cheapest option and it was the industry standard for displaying ecommerce pricing terms.

Legacy Product Detail

In the existing product detail page the user chooses the billing cycle from a dropdown menu. The billing cycle would be either monthly or annual billing. The price shown would be updated to reflect this.

New Commerce Product Detail

The thrid buying option was introduced into the product detail card. This lead to a complete redesign of this page. The user journey would now take the user from the product list into the product detail where all three buying options would be displayed. Initially the purchasing offerings were grouped by the commitment term, being either monthly or annual. The additional billing options were then displayed. This approach was tested with users in the business and was felt to be too complex, so was not chosen as a final design proposal.

Design Iteration

Final Design

In a refined design the commitment term is included in each offering and not grouped as before. Another row was also added to the cards to show particular information for that offering such as savings or flexibility. As a default the monthly commitment and billing offering is highlighted but a savings badge was introduced for the yearly billing and annual commitment offering to show the user the savings they could make if they purchased this option.

Viewing subscriptions

The new commerce experience extended the functionality for managing purchased subscriptions also. The changes that had to be brought into the ONEClub portal included:

  • Cancellation rules for subscriptions.
  • Upsizing & downsizing rules for subscriptions.
  • Auto-renewal functionality.
  • Scheduling changes to the subscription at renewal date.
  • Upgrading a subscription mid-term before renewal.

1 - Cancellations

With the new experience Microsoft enforced stricter rules relating to cancellations. Where in the past there was a 30 day window to cancel, this was changed to 7 days and after this time cancellation would not be possible until the renewal date or expiry date of the subscription. The new business rules were communicated to users by using notifications, messaging and banners in the portal.
